Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Gerber file?

A Gerber file (.gbrl) is the industry‑standard format that defines each copper layer, solder mask, silkscreen, and drill data for your PCB.

Can I upload multiple boards at once?

Yes—bundle all your panel designs into a single .zip and we’ll panelize and quote accordingly.

How long does prototyping take?

Standard turnaround is 5–7 business days. If you’re in a hurry, rush options (2–3 days) are available at checkout.

What finishes and solder mask colors are available?

We offer HASL (lead‑free), ENIG, and OSP finishes. Solder mask colors include green, red, blue, black, white, and more.

What file naming conventions should I follow?

Use the standard Gerber naming (e.g. *.GTL for top copper, *.GBL for bottom copper). If you’re unsure, zip your entire output folder and upload it.
